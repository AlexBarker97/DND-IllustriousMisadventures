Black... Darkness... Nothingness...

At first, the void did not frighten Sol. Why would it? There was nothing in it. No monster, no storm, no rival, no failure. Just endless dark.
For a man who had always laughed heartily, feared nothing, and met the world with broad shoulders and brighter confidence, it almost felt amusing. He stood in it proudly, chin high, waiting.

But nothing came... It simply continued. Nothingness...
No heat. No smell of salt or smoke on the air. No crackle of flame. No clatter of a kitchen. No laughter from a tavern.
No creak of a ship beneath his feet. No crew. No hunger.
No praise. No criticism.
Nothing.

Then Sol understood. This was not a void that threatened him with pain. It threatened him with absence. What began to terrify him was not death, but the thought of becoming nothing. Not Sol the Sunlit Chef. A blank cookbook. An unnamed trident rusting on a wall. A ship’s galley without him in it. A world that carried on as if Sol had never stood in it.

That was the fear. Not failure, but never having become anything at all.

A voice booms out all around, smothering you with its intensity.
"You are irrelevant, Solemon Smith. You are the ash that will return to ash. A life with no substance such as yours, I take with ease.

You feel your life force start to slip away, as though it was being siphoned from you by some enormous tap, draining you dry.
For a moment, that truth nearly swallowed him. Then, stubbornly, proudly, Sol answered it. 


“No... I am not afraid of failure. Let the meal burn. Let me be mocked. Let me fall short. I can learn. I can try again. I can cook again. But I will not surrender myself to becoming nothing.”

Sol raises his hand, and in his palm appears not a raging inferno, nor a grand spell worthy of ballads, but a small, steady cooking flame. A hearth-flame. Warm, golden, alive.
Sol realises, in this moment, that greatness is not in being remembered by all people forever. It is in making something real, something warm, something good, while he still has breath to do it.

Now with a steady grin on his face Sol speaks to the void “I am Sol... And it is rather difficult to overlook the sunlit chef when he is the brightest thing in the room.”
